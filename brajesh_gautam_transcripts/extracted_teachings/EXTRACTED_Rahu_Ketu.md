## III. RAHU-KETU AXIS INTERPRETATION

### The Head and Body Metaphor
From Brajesh Gautam's teachings:
- Rahu is the HEAD (Seer) - sees, plans, desires, strategizes
- Ketu is the BODY/TAIL - executes work without seeing the result
- Together they form ONE entity with opposing energies
- Rahu-Ketu axis represents the eternal tension between seeking and releasing

### Rahu as Skilled Labor (Dikhta Hai - Visible)
- Rahu has eyes; can see and plan
- Represents desires that are visible and understood
- Governs what we can perceive and pursue

### Ketu as Unskilled Labor (Andha - Blind)
- Ketu has no eyes; works blindly
- Represents detachment and surrender
- Associated with hidden wisdom and intuitive knowledge

### The Karmic Cycle
- Rahu brings obsession and intense desire
- Ketu detaches and shows the futility of those desires
- Together they create the wheel of karma and spiritual progress
